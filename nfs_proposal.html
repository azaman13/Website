Know and Understand Thyself Better

Introduction:

The goal of this project is to build a system that detects various human emotions and subtle actions such as alteration of voice (voluntary and involuntary) that may be used as a trigger mechanism for some kind of distress that a person may be undergoing. This project aims to give one the additional tool to live a healthy, conscious life by getting more insights about oneself. Using Computer Vision, Speech Recognition, and Machine Learning techniques we can train our system to understand human psychology and behaviors; and provide live feedback about an individual’s daily life actions.

Practical Usefulness:

Today almost half of Americans use smartphones [1]. We can easily use these devices around us and leverage the omnipresence of these information retrieval sources to get insights about the emotional state of an individual; and help the individual with necessary means.

As a social being we always need some kind of support from our near and dear ones. But due to various reasons (such as jobs, schools, responsibilities, and etc.) it has become quite difficult for individuals to stay close to his or her family and friends. Helping distressed friends and family, especially after some kind of traumatic life experiences such as accidents, war, social or financial victimizations. Our system will attempt to act as a support system for an user, offering some kind of live daily life performance.

Background:

Currently, there has been many ground breaking work has been done in understanding nonverbal behavior to understand and recognize complex multidimensional data involving human body language, gesture detection [2]. Furthermore, this project is encouraged by exiting novel system that couples computer algorithms with human intelligence in order to automatically sense and interpret nonverbal behavior [4][5].

Approach:

Everyday we take pictures using our smart phones/camera or engage in various activities where some form of still or moving images of ourselves are recorded. Using these images, we can track and discover nonverbal behavioral cues such as pupil movement, hand gestures, pose, and body movements.  Moreover, we can study the topology of human emotions (happy, sad, etc) by investigating the types of picture that one takes in our daily life. Alone selfies, selfies with friends, too many pictures of being in a public space within a small time period, pictures of landscape, nature, etc are various indicators of one’s personal state of mind. Using different computer vision techniques we can detect these external features and train our system to be smart enough to predict human emotions. We will be using mechanical turk to gather data for testing the accuracy of our algorithms.

There are three component to this project:

Speech Recognition: In this component we aim to use the microphone of the user’s phone and record user’s voice, specifically pitch, loudness, etc. We will discard all other information because we can predict a user’s emotional state  by monitoring the loudness, pitch, frequency of their voice.

Vision:  

For this section we aim to analyze the pictures/videos that a user is going to take in their daily life. Depending on the type of pictures/video they are taking, it may give us a bit more insights about their emotional state on a particular day. For example pictures of landscape, nature, ocean, beaches may mean the the user is in a very quiet, calm, place and is possibly in a peaceful state of mind.

Social Media component:

Nowadays, almost everyone who uses a smartphone engages in some kind of social media such as facebook, twitter, instagram, vine, pinterest, tumblr, etc. People express their emotions, opinions (which is a form of emotions), and react to other’s activities on these social media platform. We aim to capture this aspect of user’s interactivity and understand what they are going through in a particular day.

To evaluate the accuracy of the algorithms of the various components we can use the mechanical turk data as gold standard, since human judgement is the best and absolute truth.

Once we have some prototype we aim to test our prototype in places like Rochester Medical school’s Department of Psychiatry patients or even at University of Rochester’s Medical Health Center where there is a large number students come there for counseling of various types. Once our application is installed in their mobile phones we will be able to provide them live update of how they are doing in their day to day activities.

The rest of the time we will use to improve our algorithms and the application once we start getting some feedback from the Medical School and Health Center clients. Releasing this as an open source commercial product for both android and apple users is our ultimate goal. This will be a free software available for anyone.

Future Work:

There could be multiple avenues where we can take our system as future works. Social media such as Facebook has almost 175 million active users [3]; every second we are generating contents in these (Facebook, Twitter, Vine, Pinterest, etc) social media spaces. These contents can tell us a lot about users’ daily life activity, their emotional state or even sometimes can tell about one’s future actions or intentions. We can incorporate these live social media data to our systems and apply different types of information retrieval algorithms to understand these texts. It will be interesting to see how the inclusion of social media data will affect the performance of our system. We are predicting that once we include the social media data our system it will be able to provide much through and detailed information about a user’s daily self report.
